Why the timing works

You arrive the week before the Aug 12 new moon, so every evening after dark is moonless — ideal for bioluminescence and astrophotography. And the morning low tides get lower and later all week (down to −2.1 ft on departure day), so tide pooling lands at a civilized hour.

The plan brackets your long run with early nights, places a recovery massage and the birthday dinner the evening of the long run, and schedules both late nights before sleep-in mornings.

Anacortes layover · Wed ~3–7 PM

🌲 Close & easy (by the terminal)

  • Washington Park — 2 min from the ferry dock; 2.25-mi loop, beaches, Olympic + San Juan views, porpoises at Juniper Point.
  • Cap Sante overlook — quick drive-up view of the marina & islands.
  • Tommy Thompson Trail — flat, paved bay-crossing path; easy shakeout walk/jog for the legs.

⛰️ If you have extra time

  • Mount Erie — drive to the 1,273-ft summit for big views (~15 min; spotty cell service).
  • Deception Pass State Park (~20 min) — iconic bridge + Rosario Beach tide pools.
  • Buy the $30 annual Discover Pass here — it also covers Moran (Mt Constitution) & Obstruction Pass later.

🛒 Snacks & provisions

Day by day

Day 1

Wed Aug 5 · Travel day → arrive

  • Flight UA 2099 · ORD 8:45a → SEA 11:38a.
  • Pick up rental car; drive SeaTac → Anacortes (~2–2.5 hr). Grab dinner + a few snacks in Anacortes before the boat (Food Co-op before 6 PM, or Safeway open late) — Eastsound stores will be closed by the time you reach Orcas.
  • Ferry 7:50 PM Anacortes → Orcas (target; 9:25 PM backup) → Deer Harbor by ~9:30 PM.
  • Settle in. Early night — short run tomorrow. (Big grocery run is Thursday morning.)
Day 2

Thu Aug 6 · Shakeout run + Turtleback + Bioluminescence + stars

  • Run Short run (5–7 mi) out-and-back to Deer Harbor Marina. Go early; narrow roads, face traffic, bright gear.
  • Town Late-morning grocery run in Eastsound (Orcas Food Co-op + Island Market — see shopping list) + lunch: The Kitchen (tofu/tempeh bowls) or Harmony Hive Bistro (vegan wraps + smoothies).
  • Hike Turtleback Mountain (free preserve): ~6 mi, ~1,300 ft, west-facing views; closest hike to you. (Move to Fri afternoon if you'd rather a lighter day before tonight's kayak.)
  • Dinner Early dinner out — Mijitas (open Thu 4–8) or Pizzeria Portofino (vegan pizza).
  • Night Bioluminescent Kayak Tour — Orcas Wild (~10 pm–midnight): check in 1608 Rosario Rd, 30 min before your after-dark departure. Bring a red-light headlamp, warm layers, windbreaker, shoes you can get wet. Near new moon = strong glow.
  • Night Astrophotography from the property after the kayak — you're already up; Milky Way SW. Sleep in tomorrow (whale watch is midday).
Day 3

Fri Aug 7 · Whale watching

  • Sleep in and recover from last night's late kayak.
  • Tour Deer Harbor Charters whale watch (~$125/pp, ~3–4 hr; departs the marina ~2 mi from your door — book ahead, confirm time). 95%+ peak-season sighting rate.
  • Afternoon: unwind / short West Beach stroll.
  • Dinner in (relaxed). Early to bed (~9:30–10 pm) — long run at dawn.
Day 4

Sat Aug 8 · Long run + spa + Ian's 50th birthday 🎉

  • Run ~5:35 am — Long run (10–12 mi): Deer Harbor Rd → Crow Valley Rd → Eastsound and back. Done by ~7:30–8 am. Carry dates/fuel + electrolytes.
  • Late morning: breakfast, shower, easy rest; hydrate well.
  • Spa ~1–3 pm — Couples massage at Orcas Island Spa (Eastsound; the former Rosario spa team; 70 min/$135; side-by-side rooms) or A Massage by the Sea (couples massage + far-infrared sauna). Request a relaxation/recovery massage, not deep tissue (you hike + kayak tomorrow). Book ahead.
  • Dinner ~6:30 pm — Birthday dinner: Matía Kitchen, Eastsound (upscale, vegan with a heads-up call), same town as the spa. Doe Bay Café is the scenic ~35–40 min alternative — pair with a private soak in Doe Bay's saltwater tubs + sauna ($150/90 min; off-site access is last-minute only, call ahead).
  • Home by ~10 pm — Sunday is a full day.
Day 5

Sun Aug 9 · Tide pools + Mt Constitution + pack for departure

  • Tide pools Indian Island, Eastsound (low 8:34 am, −1.8 ft): walk the gravel bar across at low tide; sea stars, anemones, crabs. Return before the tide refills.
  • Hike Mt Constitution via Little Summit Trail (Moran State Park; $10 Discover Pass): ~4.5 mi, ~450–790 ft, 360° summit views. Drive up if legs are tired. Pairs with Indian Island.
  • Late afternoon: sunset stroll at West Beach; pack the car tonight for an early start.
  • Dinner in or early casual out. Early to bed — early ferry tomorrow.
Day 6

Mon Aug 10 · Early departure → catch UA 2101

  • No sleep-in. Wake ~5:00 am (car packed last night); coffee/breakfast to-go.
  • ~5:40 am leave Deer Harbor → Orcas ferry terminal; arrive by ~6:15 am (45 min before to check in).
  • Ferry 7:05 am Orcas → Anacortes → off the boat ~8:15 am.
  • Drive Anacortes → SeaTac (~2.5 hr), return rental; at SEA by ~11:00 am.
  • Flight UA 2101 · SEA 1:55 pm → ORD 8:21 pm.
  • Note: the −2.1 ft low tide (9:29 am) falls during travel — Sunday's tide pools cover it.
